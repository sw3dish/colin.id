I haven't had much time to write lately. The recent US election took up a lot of
brainpower and it's been nice to take a few weeks to unplug from tech on the weekends.

## Photography

A few weeks ago, I went to Catoctin Mountain State Park near DC with a few friends
to hike one of the easier trails there and shoot some film. It was about 50/50
regarding those wearing masks and those not, which was unfortunate -- but there weren't
that many people on the trail and my group tried to step off the trail as much as possible.
However, it did mean that we couldn't stop for long at the areas with the best views,
so no great photos of scenic Maryland vistas.

Wandering around looking for neat things to take pictures of has been a good way to
get out in DC during the pandemic. I've picked up an Olympus XA2, which as been a really
nice introductory camera to learn the exposure triangle. The biggest takeaway from
the first roll of film I've had developed is that my eye is not a great tool to measure
light and that a light meter is almost essential. Most of my photos came out blurry
(because of the slow shutter speed I was using), but that's learning a new hobby!

## Listening

released an amazing album, [*Songs*](https://adriannelenker.bandcamp.com/album/songs) in late October.
I've been on a folk kick lately (Fleet Foxes, Sufjan Stevens), so this hits all the right buttons
with wistful vocals and layered guitar.

Otherwise, I've been in a bit of a rut finding new music. There's an undulation to my
music tastes -- either I listen to exclusively Soundcloud mixes of disco/house/rap or
I listen to full albums of indie pop/folk. I've found that summer is usually the former, while
winter is the latter. Spring + fall go either way and, right now, I'm teetering on the edge but leaning
towards full albums.

## Safari

I've been more aware than usual of the age of my personal 2013 Macbook Pro recently.
My work laptop is a 16" 2019 and is speced out nicely, so the switch from it to my
personal Mac after work is... disappointing.

The new M1 Macs look nice (very nice to be honest), but I can't imagine that
they won't have some significant growing pains, **especially** for developers.

So, in lieu of a new Mac, I've been looking for ways to make daily things
a bit faster.

Since a lot of what I do is on the web, I decided to (finally) switch from Chrome to
Safari for my default browser on my Mac and my iPhone. Largely, I've been putting it off due to lack of
account switching like Google Accounts on Chrome --
mainly, I like having separate bookmarks for tech and non-tech.

I think the pros outweigh the cons here, though.
- Tab sharing between iPhone and Mac is way more useful than I thought it would be
- The privacy features are nice, but I don't really have any idea of how that's really affecting me
- My Mac seems faster without Chrome constantly running, and sites seem to load faster.
I haven't done any real testing on this, and I won't because I don't really care.
- Apple Pay integration is so so so cool. Makes it way easier to buy things I shouldn't, but it's neat.

Other neat things about the Apple ecosystem I've noticed in the last few weeks:
- Handoff is semi-useful. I'm not used to it, so I probably don't get the most out of it.
- Airpods device switching is super neat. Right now, I can only switch betwen my iPad and iPhone
as I haven't installed Big Sur, but it's almost seamless.

I'm still using Chrome on my work laptop, as it can handle having a few windows and lots of tabs
open, and I'm using it on my personal laptop for any web development that I might do, though
my side projects are leaning away from it right now.

## Pinboard

Since I'm split between Chrome and Safari now and have different Google accounts,
my bookmarks were all over the place, split across 4 different accounts with ~1000 articles in Pocket.

I wanted to have a way to access them from multiple devices and multiple browsers. I also wanted to
break them out of the Google ecosystem, as I've recently read of users being locked out of their accounts
with little chance of recovery. While the bookmarks wouldn't be the worst thing to lose, I'd still
like to not.

I chose [Pinboard](https://pinboard.in), a super simple operation run by a single developer
(whose [blog](https://idlewords.com) I really enjoy). There's a surprising amount of functionality,
but I've been using it mainly to tag links, to use as a bookmark replacement,
and mark some of those to read later, similar to Pocket. It's very low tech -- no
official iOS app, adding links is through bookmarklets (major throwback) -- but exposes
a pretty robust API, so I've only to choose 3rd party apps to supplement.

I need to get better about being more liberal with what I add to Pinboard -- I want
to use it more like Pocket, where I can just throw links in and go back later and prune back.

## Fitness

I've been running some of Jim Wendler's 5/3/1 programs for the last 4 months.
I bit off a little bit more than I should've and set too ambitious of a training max initially
(90% rather than 85%). That had me really struggling after my 4th cycle, so I recalculated maxes
and went down to 85%.

It's going well, and I've been really enjoying not having to lift maximally every workout
but still see noticable progress. I'm doing some more volume-heavy programs, which
have been very good for my bench and overhead presses. Turns out the secret to
getting over the plateau on those was to just do more reps.
