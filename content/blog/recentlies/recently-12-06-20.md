Lots of photography, not much else!

## Cameras

A friend of mine loaned me his SLR to try out, since I've been using an
Olympus XA 2, which is auto-metered, to shoot my previous 2 rolls of film.

It's an Olympus OM-2N which I hear is highly regarded in terms of design and available lenses.
I've been shooting it in aperture priority mode, rather than full manual -- selecting
both aperture and shutter speed -- since it cuts down on the factors that I need to think about
before I take my exposure. I'll definitely be using the manual mode, but I'm really trying to not
be overwhelmed with options.

The first roll I shot through it was Fuji Superia 400, which is one of the
few consumer film stocks that I've been able to find affordably.
Prices have really been skyrocketing on film lately! Not sure why -- though
I seemed to have picked a poor time in terms of film options to pick up this hobby.
Many companies are cutting down on the number of stocks they offer and most that are
still produced are out of stock.

Unfortunately, this new camera loads a bit differently than my XA 2 and I didn't
seat the film on the sprockets correctly!
I "shot" a whole roll of film, went to have it developed,
then realized that I had 36 perfectly blank exposures.

I've learned my lesson though -- I've realized that the rewind spool should be
turning every time I advance to a new exposure and I'll be watching it
eagle-eyed for a few rolls.

Funnily, I didn't have the same problem on the XA 2, though I think that it could've
happened still.

Speaking of the XA 2, I was lucky enough to get my hands on its older sister, the
XA. My dad was in the Peace Corps in the 80s and picked one up to take pictures in Tunisia,
so it has a spot of provenance.

It's surprisingly much more fun to shoot than the XA 2. The control you
have over the aperture and focus make it a very capable camera in such a small
form factor. I've been walking the streets of DC, shooting Fuji Superia 400 in the mornings
and evenings. I'm making sure to watch the shutter speed indicator to make sure I won't
get any camera shake from shooting in low light -- it's a bit easier to notice than the
green LED low light indicator on the XA 2.

In the SLR, I am trying my first roll of black and white film -- Kodak T-Max 400, which
I'm pushing to 1600 ISO to do a little low light shooting in the late evenings.

I've picked up some rolls of Cinestill 800-T as gifts for my friends who got me into
photography -- and a roll for myself, as well as a roll of 80-D. I think they'll be
very fun to shoot, especially the 800-T. I might get a tripod and find some gas stations
to shoot at night, as going with the trend.


## Deload week

I've just finished off another two cycles of Boring but Big by Jim Wendler. That marks
the end of the two leaders and the beginning of the anchor cycle. Generally, the leaders
are for volume and the anchors are for strength. I'm running the First Set Last
anchor program which incorporates a 5x5 set of the first working set, which you do last in your
programmed lifts.
