> **Le mieux est l'ennemi du bien - Voltaire**

I meant to write more in 2019.
Since 2018 had been dry on the writing front, I meant to write **anything**.

But first, I needed a static site generator.

Jekyll? *Boring.*

Hugo? *I don't know Go (this site is built on Hugo, so I evidently I got over it).*

Gatsby? *Too much React for a blog.*

Eleventy? Serum? Hexo? (thanks [StaticGen](https://staticgen.com) for the choice overload.)

I thought -- *I'm a dev, why don't I make one?*

\~\~Cue hacking noises (like an aggressive cough)\~\~

Well, after 6+ months of trying to hack in between work and freelancing, I've
written part of a [static site engine](https://www.github.com/sw3dish/graaff) and exactly **zero** words.

And while I learned a lot building `graaff`, I realized:

> **Perfect is the enemy of good.** (that's what the French at the top says)

In this case, having the best static site generator kept me from putting anything on the page.
Or at least the metaphorical page. I let myself be sidetracked from my original goal, and
moved the goalposts ***on myself***.

And this isn't without precedent.

When I have an idea for a side project,
I'll spend so much time in the config or down a docs hole worrying if the package I chose
will fit version 23, that my momentum to write actual code evaporates.

So for 2020, I'll not let perfection stop me from getting something on the page.
From writing something that compiles. From achieving 75% of a goal. I'll step back,
channel my inner Voltaire, and take the first step, even if I don't have the best shoes.

**I'll just start.**

And this blog is that first step for my writing. It's built with [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/),
whose docs scared me away in the past, and [Pulp](https://github.com/koirand/pulp),
a theme that does 85% of what I want.

Neither make up the platonic ideal of my blog, but that's alright.
It does what it needs to -- present my ideas -- and took 15 minutes
to set up.

And I can tell that this'll be a long road. Ignoring the nagging voice that is **perfectionism**
will take time.

Even when writing this post, I felt the urge to tweak the typography and
investigate why there's a `bundle.js` generated for a page of just text
(see me after my talk about my opinions on unnecessary JS).

But for now, I'll leave you with what I hope will become my inner monologue in 2020.

> **Perfect?** *Sure.* **Good?** *Great.*
