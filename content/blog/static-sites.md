In the beginning, there were static sites.

Or at least that's what people tell me, since my first computer ran on Windows Vista
and I first wrote HTML in `.php` files (I was in high school, so put the pitchforks down).

In this age of React, Vue, and Svelte, why have a site that is just basic HTML?

My first foray into building static sites wasn't a static site generator.
[Plateau](https://github.com/sw3dish/plateau) took `.md` files and, on every request,
generated HTML to be served. I think it might've been my web app I wrote from scratch,
and maximal performance wasn't the first thing on my to-do list.
