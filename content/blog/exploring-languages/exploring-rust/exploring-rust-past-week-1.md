(I'm doing a pretty bad job of jotting down notes on my learning Rust 😬. Trying to do a better job, but busy at work!)

Over the last weeks, I've been watching some videos on Rust to go along with ["The Book"](https://doc.rust-lang.org/book/):

- [Rust: A Language for the Next 40 Years](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A3AdN7U24iU) by Carol Nichols
- [Why is Rust Successful?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-Tj8Q12DaEQ) by Florian Gilcher
- [Rust, WebAssembly, and the future of Serverless](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-2BO43FvBuA) by Steve Klabnik

I also read [Why Rust](https://www.oreilly.com/programming/free/files/why-rust.pdf) and started working through
[Rustlings](https://github.com/rust-lang/rustlings), which are like the Ruby Koans.

I also took a look at [Are We Web Yet](https://www.arewewebyet.org) since that's where my interest lies primarily, though
[Are We Game Yet](https://arewegameyet.rs) is on my radar as well.

From "The Book", I read chapters 4 - 9 which cover:

- Ownership (briefly, just the idea of references and borrowing)
- Structs
- Enums and pattern matching
- Modules/crates
- Collections (Vec, HashMap, String)
- Error handling

Honestly, it's been pretty difficult to remember about references and dereferencing,
mutability and the borrow checker. I'm going to stick with it, though -- I'll at least finish "The Book".
I've found some really good resources on Rust and I think making a more fully-fledged
(yet still reasonably small) program will help me work out some of my confusion.
