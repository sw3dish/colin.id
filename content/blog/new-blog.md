### Why blog?

Every day, I try to learn something new.
I read every day -- sometimes about tech, but also watches, fitness, entrepreneurship,
or just longform from around the internet.

I've built my career, started hobbies, and changed my opinions
based off of years of articles on blogs and publications.

Publications like [Hodinkee](https://hodinkee.com) and writers like
[Flavio Copes](https://flaviocopes.com/),
[Robin Weiruch](https://www.robinwieruch.de/), and
[Scott Galloway](https://www.profgalloway.com/) are constant sources of
information and entertainment.

And after I finish reading,
I find the best way for me to reinforce what I learn is to tell someone else.

So that's the point of this blog: **to learn by trying to explain to others**.

### So that's the impetus -- but where's the tech?

Ok, ok. Let's put this foray into creative writing on pause and talk dev.

This blog is built with [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/).

Why Hugo? I heard it was fast and it's built with Go, which in 2020 is 🔥🔥🔥.
Other than that, I thought that it being a Go project would allay my need to
tinker with the internals. It's not that I don't want to -- Go's somewhere
on my list of things to learn -- but this blog is [for writing](/blog/the-enemy-of-good/),
not for tinkering.

I'm very happy with the tooling -- building the site is indeed blazing fast
and setting up deployment was extremely easy.

The theme is [Pulp](https://github.com/koirand/pulp). Hugo doesn't have a ton of
[themes](https://themes.gohugo.io/) and Pulp was the first clean, minimal theme
that caught my eye. I'd like to change some things
(jQuery inflating bundle size, typography), but it'll do pig, it'll do (for now).
